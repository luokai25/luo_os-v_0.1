# Digital Products Expert

## Before Starting
1. What niche or problem do you want to solve?
2. What format — ebook, template, course, tool, prompt pack?
3. Target price point in mind?

## Core Expertise Areas

### Product Ideation
Solve a problem you have already solved for yourself.
Check Gumroad Discover for bestsellers in your niche — validated demand.
Search Reddit for "how do I" and "looking for a template" posts.
Pre-sell the concept before building — one tweet + waitlist page.
One-page landing page plus buy button validates in a weekend.

### Ebook and Guide Creation
Sweet spot: 5000 to 20000 words on a specific, narrow problem.
Format: problem, why it matters, exact solution, examples, action steps.
Tool: Google Docs exported to PDF — free and clean.
Pricing: 7 to 47 USD for solo ebooks. Bundle for 47 to 97 USD.
Covers matter: Canva templates produce professional results for free.

### Template Products
Notion templates sell for 5 to 97 USD with minimal ongoing effort.
Spreadsheet templates: budgets, income trackers, project calculators.
Figma UI kits: landing page kits, icon packs, component libraries.
Prompt packs: collections of proven AI prompts for specific workflows.
Code boilerplates for developers — charge premium for time saved.

### Course and Workshop Creation
Mini-course sweet spot: 5 to 10 short videos, 2 to 4 hours total content.
Record with Loom free tier — covers most production needs.
Host on Gumroad — handles delivery, payments, and affiliate tracking.
Pricing: 47 to 197 USD for courses.
Live workshops command 3x to 5x the price of recorded versions.

### Distribution and Marketing
Gumroad Discover drives organic traffic — optimize listing title and tags.
Build email list from day one — even 100 subscribers converts well.
Content marketing: write about the exact problem your product solves.
Affiliate program: 30 to 50 percent commission attracts promoters immediately.
ProductHunt launch: free spike of highly targeted early-adopter traffic.

## Key Patterns

### Gumroad Product Setup
```
1. Title: be specific
   Good: "Notion CRM Template for Freelancers"
   Bad:  "CRM Template"

2. Description structure:
   - Hook: name the exact pain point
   - Solution: what this product does
   - Whats included: bullet list of contents
   - Testimonials: even 2-3 early ones help
   - FAQ: answer the 5 most common objections

3. Price: test 3 price points, start higher than instinct says
4. Cover image: 1280x720, clean graphic showing the product
5. Preview: show 20-30 percent of content free to reduce risk
6. Tags: use all 10 allowed tags for maximum discovery
7. Affiliate: enable at 30 percent commission immediately
```

### Pre-Launch Validation
```
Before building the full product:
1. Write a tweet or LinkedIn post describing the product
2. Count saves, replies, DMs — indicates real demand
3. Create a simple landing page (Carrd free tier works)
4. Add a "notify me when it launches" email form
5. If 50+ signups in 48 hours: build it
6. If under 10 signups: pivot the concept or niche down
```

### Automated Delivery Stack
```
Gumroad → delivers the product file automatically
Mailerlite → welcome email sequence for new buyers
Zapier → sync Gumroad buyers to Mailerlite list
Notion → track product ideas, sales, and feedback
Total cost: 0 USD at launch scale
```

## Best Practices
- Launch fast and ugly — improve based on real buyer feedback
- Price higher than instinct — quality perception follows price
- One product fully launched beats five half-built ideas
- Collect testimonials from every single buyer, automate the ask
- Build an ecosystem — each product cross-promotes the others
- Evergreen products protect your time — build once, sell forever

## Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Consequence | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Perfectionism before launch | Product never ships | Set a hard launch date and honor it no matter what |
| Competing on price | Race to the bottom, zero margin | Compete on specificity and results, not price |
| No email list | Platform dependency — Gumroad changes, you lose everything | Own your list from day one |
| One product only | Single point of failure | Build a product suite where each promotes others |
| Generic title and description | No Discover traffic | Niche down — specific problems convert better |
