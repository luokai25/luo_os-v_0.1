# Freelance and Consulting Expert

## Before Starting
1. What is your core skill or service?
2. Target client type — individuals, small business, or enterprise?
3. Income target — survival, side income, or full-time replacement?

## Core Expertise Areas

### Positioning and Niche
Generalists compete on price. Specialists command premium rates.
Niche formula: [skill] for [specific industry] who want [specific outcome].
Example: "React development for SaaS startups who need to ship fast."
Strong positioning makes client acquisition passive over time.
Own one niche completely before expanding to adjacent ones.

### Pricing Strategy
Hourly: easiest to sell, caps your income — avoid long-term.
Project-based: win-win for efficiency — you earn more when you are fast.
Retainer: predictable income — best for ongoing relationships.
Value-based: price on ROI to client, not your time — highest ceiling.
Rate anchoring: always present three tiers — most clients choose middle.

### Client Acquisition
Warm outreach converts 10x better than cold — start with your network.
LinkedIn: optimize profile for inbound, post content in your niche weekly.
Reddit: answer questions in your target client's communities — no pitching.
Referrals: ask every happy client for two referral introductions.
Content: one detailed case study per project builds better than ads.

### Proposals and Closing
Lead with problem and impact, not your credentials.
Include three options: basic, standard, premium.
Specify exactly what is included and what is not.
Show social proof — past clients, results, numbers.
Use async video (Loom) for proposal walkthroughs — dramatically higher close rate.

### Contracts and Payments
Always use a contract — even for small projects.
Key clauses: scope, payment schedule, revisions, IP ownership, kill fee.
Payment upfront: 50 percent deposit before starting, 50 percent on delivery.
Use Stripe or PayPal for professionalism and payment protection.
Late payment clause: 1.5 percent per month on overdue invoices.

## Key Patterns

### Positioning Statement Template
```
I help [specific client type]
who struggle with [specific problem]
achieve [specific outcome]
without [common obstacle or fear].

Example:
I help early-stage SaaS founders
who struggle with slow frontend development
ship production-ready React apps in weeks
without hiring a full-time engineer.
```

### Three-Tier Proposal
```
Option A — Essential (lowest price)
Core deliverable only
Defined scope, minimal revisions
Good for: clients testing the relationship

Option B — Standard (mid price — highlight this one)
Full scope plus one round of revisions
Priority communication
Recommended for: most clients

Option C — Premium (highest price)
Full scope plus ongoing support
Weekly check-ins
Emergency availability
Good for: clients who value speed and access
```

### Client Onboarding Checklist
```
Before starting any project:
[ ] Signed contract on file
[ ] Deposit received
[ ] Project brief documented
[ ] Access credentials shared securely
[ ] Timeline agreed and in writing
[ ] Communication channel established
[ ] First milestone date set
```

## Best Practices
- Specialize deeply — it is the only way to escape commodity pricing
- Always have 3 active client conversations in pipeline — prevents feast and famine
- Raise rates by 20 percent every 6 months until resistance
- Document everything in writing — verbal agreements cause disputes
- Fire clients who do not respect boundaries — they consume all your energy

## Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Consequence | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Competing as a generalist | Constant price pressure | Pick a niche and own it |
| Hourly billing only | Income capped by hours | Move to project and retainer pricing |
| No contract | Scope creep, non-payment | Always use a contract, even for friends |
| Waiting for referrals passively | Feast and famine cycle | Ask for referrals systematically after every win |
| Undercharging | Attracting difficult low-budget clients | Price higher, attract better clients |
